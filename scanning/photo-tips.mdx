---
title: "Tips for Getting Accurate Skincare Ingredient Label Scans"
description: "Improve OCR accuracy when scanning skincare product labels by optimizing lighting, camera angle, and technique before you capture the image."
---

The app uses optical character recognition (OCR) to read ingredient text directly from a photo. OCR accuracy depends heavily on lighting conditions, how steady you hold the camera, and the physical quality of the label itself. Following the tips below significantly reduces recognition errors and saves you from manually correcting the extracted list afterward.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Use natural or bright indoor lighting">
    Good lighting is the single biggest factor in scan quality. Dim lighting forces the camera to increase sensor sensitivity, which introduces grain and makes text harder to distinguish. Harsh directional light — like a bare overhead bulb directly above the label — creates strong shadows that obscure characters.

    Indirect natural daylight from a window works best. If you're indoors without good window light, use a bright overhead light combined with a secondary light source to fill in shadows. Avoid scanning under warm incandescent bulbs alone, as they shift white balance and reduce contrast on white labels.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Hold the phone steady">
    Even slight camera movement during capture produces motion blur, which makes individual characters unreadable. The app captures automatically when it detects a stable image, so an unsteady hand can cause repeated failed attempts or blurry captures that slip through.

    Prop your elbow on a table or counter to stabilize your arm. Alternatively, set the product flat on a surface and hold the phone above it with both hands. If the product is small, place it on a contrasting background — dark surface for light-colored labels, light surface for dark ones — so the camera can clearly distinguish the label edge.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Fill the frame with the ingredient list">
    The app performs best when the ingredient list fills most of the viewfinder, with minimal surrounding label content in the frame. Logos, product names, marketing copy, and decorative artwork around the ingredient section can confuse the text detector and reduce the accuracy of the crop.

    Move the camera closer until the ingredient block fills roughly 70–80% of the screen. If the full ingredient list is too long to fit in one frame, you can capture it in two overlapping sections — the app will prompt you to merge them.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Avoid glare from shiny packaging">
    Glossy or metallic label finishes reflect light back into the camera, washing out the text underneath. This is especially common with premium serums and glass bottle packaging where the label has a lacquered or foil finish.

    Tilt the product a few degrees — anywhere from 10 to 30 degrees — away from the dominant light source to shift the reflection off the text area. Small adjustments in tilt angle make a noticeable difference. You don't need to eliminate all glare, just move it away from the printed ingredient text.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Clean the lens">
    A smudged or greasy camera lens softens the entire image, reducing sharpness even in good lighting. Skincare products in particular make lens contamination more likely — handling creams, oils, or serums before picking up your phone can transfer residue to the lens.

    Wipe the lens with a soft, lint-free cloth before scanning. A clean microfiber cloth works well; avoid paper towels or clothing fabric that can scratch the lens coating. Check the lens under a light to confirm it's clean before you scan.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Try multiple angles">
    If the first scan captures an incomplete or garbled ingredient list, don't enter edit mode immediately — try scanning again from a slightly different angle first. Small changes in the camera's position relative to the label can resolve issues caused by label curvature, uneven surfaces, or micro-shadows between raised label edges and the packaging.

    Try tilting the camera 10–15 degrees to one side, or moving slightly left or right while keeping the label centered in the frame. Two or three attempts from different angles usually resolves persistent recognition issues without needing manual correction.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
  After every scan, the app shows you the extracted ingredient list before running analysis. You can tap any ingredient to edit it inline, delete duplicates, or add ingredients the scan missed. Always review this list before tapping Analyze — correcting OCR errors here ensures your safety rating is based on accurate data.
</Note>

<Tip>
  If the ingredient text on a label is smaller than 6pt or the label is physically damaged, camera scanning may not produce reliable results regardless of technique. In those cases, use the manual entry option: tap **Enter Manually** from the scan screen and type or paste the ingredient list directly. You can also copy the ingredient list from the brand's website if the product is sold online. See [Enter Ingredients Manually](/scanning/manual-entry) for the full workflow.
</Tip>
