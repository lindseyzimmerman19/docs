---
title: "How to Scan a Skincare Product Label or Barcode for Analysis"
description: "Learn how to scan a skincare product's ingredient label or barcode to instantly analyze ingredients and get your personalized safety rating."
---

The Skincare Ingredient Scanner gives you two ways to pull ingredient data from a product: point your camera at the ingredient list printed on the label, or scan the barcode to look the product up in the app's database. Both methods lead to the same analysis — choose whichever fits your situation best.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Camera Scan">
    Use the camera scan when you have the physical product in hand and want to capture the full ingredient list directly from the label. This method works for any product, whether or not it's in the app's database.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the app and tap Scan">
        Launch the Skincare Ingredient Scanner. On the home screen, tap the **Scan** button at the bottom of the screen to open the camera view.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Point the camera at the ingredient list">
        Flip the product to the back panel and locate the ingredient list — it's usually printed in small text near the bottom or side of the label. Aim the camera directly at this section, not at the front branding or barcode.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Hold steady until the app captures the text">
        Keep your hand as still as possible. The app outlines the text region in real time and automatically captures when it detects a clear, stable image. You'll feel a short vibration and see a confirmation animation when the capture is complete.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Review the extracted ingredient list">
        The app displays the ingredients it recognized from the photo. Read through the list and correct any errors — OCR isn't perfect, especially on small or curved labels. Tap any ingredient name to edit it inline.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Tap Analyze">
        Once the ingredient list looks correct, tap **Analyze**. The app scores each ingredient against your skin profile and flags any potential irritants or allergens.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Barcode Scan">
    Use the barcode scan when you want a quick lookup without manually reading the label. This works best for well-known or widely distributed products that are already in the app's ingredient database.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the app and tap Scan">
        Launch the Skincare Ingredient Scanner and tap **Scan** on the home screen to open the camera view.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Switch to barcode mode">
        In the camera view, tap the **Barcode** icon in the top-right corner to switch from text capture mode to barcode scanning mode. The viewfinder changes to show a narrow horizontal frame.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Align the barcode in the frame">
        Hold the product so the barcode fits within the frame. Keep the barcode parallel to the horizontal guides. The app scans automatically once it reads the code — no need to tap a button.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Review the ingredient list from the database">
        If the product is in the database, the app loads the full ingredient list and displays the product name, brand, and formulation date where available. Review the ingredients to confirm they match the product you're holding.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Tap Analyze">
        Tap **Analyze** to run the full ingredient analysis against your skin profile.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Note>
      Not every product is in the barcode database. If the app doesn't recognize a barcode, it prompts you to enter the ingredient list manually. You can type or paste the list directly into the text field and continue to analysis without losing your progress. See [Enter Ingredients Manually](/scanning/manual-entry) for details.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Save results to your product history

After any analysis — whether from a camera scan, barcode scan, or manual entry — tap **Save to History** on the results screen to store the product in your personal product history. Saved products are available for quick re-analysis if you update your skin profile or sensitivities later.

  Getting blurry or incomplete scans from the camera? Read [Tips for Accurate Ingredient Label Scans](/scanning/photo-tips) for guidance on lighting, positioning, and handling reflective packaging.
</Tip>
