---
title: "Enter a Product's Ingredients Manually for Analysis"
description: "Type or paste a product's ingredient list directly into the app to run a full analysis when scanning isn't practical or accurate enough."
---

Manual entry lets you analyze any product's ingredients without using the camera or barcode scanner. It's particularly useful when you're shopping online and don't have the product in front of you, when the label text is too small or damaged for reliable OCR, or when you want to analyze a product before purchasing it by copying the ingredient list from a brand's website.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the app and tap the New Scan button">
    Launch the Skincare Ingredient Scanner. On the home screen, tap the **+** button or the **New Scan** button — both open the scan options screen. You don't need to grant camera permission for manual entry.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Enter Manually">
    On the scan options screen, select **Enter Manually**. This opens a text input screen with a large text field and a format hint showing the expected input style.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste or type the ingredient list">
    Paste or type the full ingredient list into the text field. Use the standard INCI format — ingredients listed in order of concentration, separated by commas. For example:

    ```text
    Aqua, Glycerin, Niacinamide, Dimethicone, Cetearyl Alcohol, Phenoxyethanol
    ```

    You don't need to clean up formatting before pasting. The app strips extra whitespace, line breaks, and common formatting artifacts automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Analyze">
    Tap **Analyze** to run the full ingredient analysis. The app matches each ingredient against its database, applies your skin profile and sensitivity settings, and displays a safety rating with a breakdown of flagged and approved ingredients.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Cosmetic ingredient labels follow the International Nomenclature of Cosmetic Ingredients (INCI) standard, which assigns standardized Latin or chemical names to every ingredient. For example, water is listed as "Aqua" and vitamin C as "Ascorbic Acid." The app's database is built on INCI names, so it recognizes INCI-formatted lists with high accuracy. It also recognizes many common names and trade names — for instance, "Vitamin E" resolves correctly to "Tocopherol" — so you don't need to convert ingredient names before entering them.
</Info>

<Tip>
  Most skincare brands publish complete ingredient lists on their product pages. Open the brand's website or the product listing on a retailer's site, find the ingredient section, and copy the full text. Paste it directly into the app's manual entry field. This approach is faster than typing and more accurate than scanning very small label text, and it works entirely from your phone while you're browsing or shopping.
</Tip>

<Note>
  After analyzing a manually entered product, tap **Save to History** on the results screen to store it in your product history. You can give the product a custom name — useful when you're comparing multiple unlabeled samples or tracking products by a nickname rather than their full INCI list. Saved products are available for quick re-analysis whenever you update your skin profile.
</Note>
