Getting up and running with Skincare Ingredient Scanner takes less than five minutes. This guide walks you through downloading the app, creating your account, and completing your first scan. By the end, you'll have a full ingredient breakdown of a product in your hand — with safety scores, concern flags, and plain-language explanations tailored to your skin.

## Prerequisites

Before you start, make sure you have:

- A smartphone running iOS 15 or later, or Android 10 or later
- The Skincare Ingredient Scanner app downloaded from the App Store or Google Play

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download the app">
    Open the App Store (iPhone) or Google Play Store (Android) and search for **Skincare Ingredient Scanner**. Tap **Get** or **Install** and wait for the download to complete. The app requires camera permission to scan labels — you'll be prompted to grant this when you first use the scanner.

    <Tip>
      When the app asks for camera access, tap **Allow**. Without camera permission, you won't be able to scan ingredient labels or barcodes. You can always update this later in your device's Settings under Privacy > Camera.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your account">
    Open the app and tap **Get started**. You can sign up with an email address and password, or use **Continue with Google** or **Continue with Apple** for faster setup. If you use social login, the app will ask you to confirm a display name on first launch.

    Your account stores your scan history, skin profile, and saved products — all synced across devices if you sign in on a second phone or tablet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up your skin profile">
    After creating your account, the app prompts you to set up your skin profile. Tap **Set up profile** and complete the following:

    - **Skin type** — choose from dry, oily, combination, sensitive, or normal
    - **Known allergens or sensitivities** — add any ingredients or ingredient families you react to (for example, fragrance, nickel, lanolin, or specific preservatives)
    - **Skin conditions** — optionally flag conditions such as rosacea, eczema, or acne-prone skin

    <Note>
      Setting up a skin profile is optional, but results are significantly more useful when you do. Without a profile, you'll still see ingredient safety ratings, but the app won't know which concerns are most relevant to you, so personalized alerts won't appear.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scan your first product">
    From the home screen, tap the **Scan** button (the camera icon at the bottom center of the screen). Point your camera at the ingredient list on the back or side of a product. Hold the camera steady — the app highlights detected text in real time and triggers the analysis automatically once it reads a valid ingredient list.

    You can also tap **Scan barcode** to point your camera at a product barcode instead. If the product is in the app's product database, the ingredient list is loaded automatically without needing to read the label text.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read your results">
    Once the scan completes, the app displays a full ingredient breakdown. Each ingredient appears with:

    - A **safety rating** — green (low concern), yellow (moderate concern), or red (high concern or known irritant)
    - A **plain-language name** alongside the INCI name
    - Any **concern flags** relevant to your skin profile, such as "known fragrance allergen" or "may aggravate rosacea"

    Tap any ingredient to open its detail card, which explains what the ingredient does in a formula and why it received its rating. Flagged ingredients are grouped at the top of the list for quick review.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Can't scan a label?

If the ingredient text on a label is too small, smudged, or printed on a curved surface, the camera scan may not pick it up cleanly. In that case, use [manual entry](/scanning/manual-entry) to paste or type the ingredient list directly. Manual entry accepts the full ingredient string as it appears on the label, and the analysis works identically to a camera scan.
