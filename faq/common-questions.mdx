This page answers the questions we hear most often from users. If you don't find what you're looking for here, reach out through **Profile → Help → Contact Us**.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is Skincare Ingredient Scanner free?">
    Skincare Ingredient Scanner offers a free tier that gives you access to core features at no cost. On the free plan, you can scan up to 10 products per month, view basic safety ratings, and flag common irritants based on a general skin profile.

    Upgrading to Premium unlocks unlimited scans, full ingredient-by-ingredient analysis reports, advanced personalization based on your specific skin type and sensitivities, and priority access to newly added product data. You can upgrade at any time from **Profile → Subscription**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How accurate is the ingredient analysis?">
    Our ingredient database is compiled from peer-reviewed dermatological and toxicological literature and is updated regularly to reflect new research. For most products, accuracy is high — but two factors affect results.

    First, scan quality matters. If the label is blurry, crumpled, or poorly lit, the optical character recognition (OCR) may extract ingredients incorrectly. Second, some labels use unusual formatting or fonts that can cause extraction errors. To address this, the app lets you review and edit the extracted ingredient list before running analysis — tap any ingredient in the results preview to correct it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is INCI and why do labels use those names?">
    INCI stands for International Nomenclature of Cosmetic Ingredients. It is the globally standardized system for naming cosmetic and personal care ingredients on product labels. Regulatory bodies in the US, EU, and many other regions require manufacturers to list ingredients using INCI names, which is why you'll see terms like *Aqua* instead of water, or *Tocopherol* instead of vitamin E.

    Skincare Ingredient Scanner recognizes INCI names automatically and translates them into plain-language equivalents in your results. You'll see both the INCI name (as it appears on the label) and a common name, so you always know exactly what you're looking at.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why does the app flag an ingredient as 'Caution' when it appears in products marketed for sensitive skin?">
    Safety ratings in Skincare Ingredient Scanner reflect population-level evidence — they summarize how an ingredient performs across a broad range of people based on available research. An ingredient rated "Caution" may cause reactions in a meaningful percentage of users, even if many people tolerate it without issue.

    Brands that market products for sensitive skin may use that same ingredient at a lower concentration that minimizes risk for most users, or they may rely on consumer testing rather than clinical evidence. Individual responses also vary significantly based on genetics, existing skin conditions, and other products you use. Use the app's ratings as a starting point for informed decisions, not as a final verdict on what will or won't work for your skin.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I trust the results to make medical decisions?">
    Skincare Ingredient Scanner provides educational information to help you understand what's in your products — it does not provide medical advice, diagnoses, or treatment recommendations.

    If you have a diagnosed skin condition such as eczema, rosacea, or contact dermatitis, or if you have confirmed allergies to specific ingredients, consult a board-certified dermatologist before making changes to your skincare routine based on the app's results. The app is a helpful research tool, not a substitute for professional medical guidance.

    <Warning>
      Do not use the app's results to manage a known allergy or a medically diagnosed skin condition without first consulting a dermatologist.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does the app work offline?">
    Partial offline functionality is available. Barcode lookup requires an active internet connection because it queries our live product database. However, ingredient analysis using a manually entered ingredient list can work offline once you have downloaded the ingredient database to your device.

    To download the database for offline use, go to **Profile → Settings → Offline Mode → Download Database**. The download is approximately 80 MB and updates automatically when you reconnect to Wi-Fi.

    <Tip>
      Download the database before traveling so you can scan products without relying on mobile data.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I add a product that isn't in the barcode database?">
    If a barcode scan returns no results, you have three options.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Switch to camera scan mode">
        Tap the camera icon on the scan screen and point your camera at the ingredient list printed on the product label. The app will extract the ingredients using OCR.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enter ingredients manually">
        Tap **Enter manually** on the scan screen and type or paste the ingredient list. Separate ingredients with commas, exactly as they appear on the label.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Suggest the product for our database">
        After scanning, tap **Suggest this product** to send the product details to our curation team. We review submissions regularly and add verified products to the database.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is my scan history private?">
    Yes. Your scan history, skin profile, sensitivity settings, and any notes you add are stored privately within your account. We do not sell, share, or license your personal data to third parties, including brands or advertisers.

    Aggregate, anonymized usage data (such as which ingredients are scanned most frequently) may be used internally to improve the app. This data cannot be linked back to individual users.

    For full details, review the Privacy Policy in the app under **Profile → Settings → Privacy Policy**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I delete my account and data?">
    You can permanently delete your account and all associated data at any time.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open your profile">
        Tap **Profile** in the bottom navigation bar.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Navigate to account settings">
        Go to **Settings → Account**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Delete your account">
        Tap **Delete Account** and confirm when prompted.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Warning>
      Account deletion is permanent and cannot be undone. All your scan history, skin profile, and saved products will be removed immediately and cannot be recovered.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>
