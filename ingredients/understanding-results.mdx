description: "Learn how to read the results screen after scanning a product, from the overall score to individual ingredient details and concern flags."
---

After you scan a product label, the app processes every ingredient and presents a results screen that gives you a complete picture of what you're putting on your skin. Each piece of information is organized to help you quickly spot what matters most for your skin type and then dig deeper into any ingredient you want to understand better.

## Results screen layout

The results screen is divided into two main sections: a summary at the top and a full ingredient breakdown below.

### Overall product score

The first thing you see is the overall product score, displayed as a color-coded indicator and a numeric value from 1 to 10. The score is an aggregate of every ingredient's individual safety rating, weighted by concentration order. A green score (1–3) means the product is generally well-tolerated; yellow (4–6) signals that one or more ingredients may warrant attention for some skin types; red (7–10) means the product contains ingredients with significant known concerns.

### Ingredient list

Below the score, the full ingredient list appears in the same order as it does on the product label — highest concentration first, descending to trace amounts. For each ingredient you see:

- **Safety rating** — a color-coded badge (green, yellow, or red) with its numeric score
- **Common name** — the everyday name for the ingredient where it differs from the INCI (International Nomenclature of Cosmetic Ingredients) name shown on the label; for example, "Vitamin C" appears alongside "Ascorbic Acid"
- **Function** — the role the ingredient plays in the formula, such as emollient, humectant, preservative, fragrance, or surfactant
- **Concern badges** — highlighted tags on any ingredient that triggered a flag based on your skin profile, such as "Comedogenic" or "Fragrance"

<Note>
Ingredient order on a product label is regulated — manufacturers must list ingredients from highest to lowest concentration. The app preserves this ordering so you can see not just which ingredients are present, but how prominent they are in the formula.
</Note>

### Ingredient detail view

Tap any ingredient in the list to open its full detail view. This screen includes:

<Steps>
  <Step title="What it is">
    A plain-language explanation of the ingredient's origin and chemical classification — whether it's a synthetic polymer, a plant-derived oil, a mineral, or a lab-produced peptide.
  </Step>
  <Step title="What it does">
    The ingredient's primary function in skincare formulas, plus any secondary effects. For example, glycerin is primarily a humectant that draws moisture into the skin but also has mild emollient properties.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Safety rating">
    The ingredient's numeric rating (1–10) and the color band it falls into, along with a brief summary of what drives that score — such as known sensitization rates or regulatory status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Known concerns">
    A list of any concern categories the ingredient is tagged with, such as fragrance, potential allergen, or comedogenic. Each concern links to a detailed explanation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prevalence">
    How commonly the ingredient appears across products in the app's database, expressed as a percentage. This gives you context about how widespread exposure to it tends to be.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Your skin flag">
    A clear indicator of whether this ingredient is flagged specifically for your skin type and sensitivities based on your profile. If your profile isn't set up yet, you'll see a prompt to complete it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Sharing and exporting results

Use the **Share** button at the top right of the results screen to export your analysis. You can share a summary as an image, copy a plain-text ingredient breakdown, or save a full PDF report. This is useful for bringing information to a dermatologist appointment or comparing products side by side.

<Tip>
Use the filter bar at the top of the ingredient list to narrow down what you're looking at. You can filter to show only flagged ingredients, or filter by a specific concern category — such as viewing only the fragrances or only the preservatives in a formula.
</Tip>
