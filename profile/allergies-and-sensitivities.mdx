Beyond skin type, you can tell the app about specific ingredients or ingredient categories you want to avoid. When a product contains something on your list, a prominent alert appears at the top of your scan results — you won't need to hunt through the ingredient list to find it. This is useful for ingredients your doctor has told you to avoid, known triggers from past reactions, or categories you've decided to cut from your routine entirely.

## Two ways to add sensitivities

**By ingredient name** — Search for a specific INCI ingredient and add it to your list. This is useful when you know the exact compound to avoid, such as `benzyl alcohol`, `lanolin`, or `sodium lauryl sulfate`. INCI names are the standardized ingredient names used on product labels globally.

**By category** — Add an entire concern category to catch a broad group of related ingredients at once. Available categories include:

- All Fragrances — catches both synthetic fragrance compounds and natural fragrance blends
- All Parabens — catches methylparaben, propylparaben, butylparaben, and all other paraben variants
- Sulfates — catches sodium lauryl sulfate, sodium laureth sulfate, and related compounds
- Essential Oils — catches plant-derived oils that can cause sensitization
- Formaldehyde Releasers — catches preservatives that slowly release formaldehyde
- Alcohol-Based Ingredients — catches alcohol denat. and similar drying alcohols (fatty alcohols like cetyl alcohol are excluded)

## Add a sensitivity to your profile

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your Profile tab">
    Tap the **Profile** icon in the bottom navigation bar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap 'Allergies & Sensitivities'">
    Under the "My Skin" section, tap **Allergies & Sensitivities** to open your sensitivity list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the '+' button">
    Tap the **+** button in the top-right corner of the screen to open the search and browse interface.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search or browse">
    Type an ingredient name in the search bar to find a specific INCI ingredient, or scroll down to browse the predefined categories. Results appear as you type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select an ingredient or category">
    Tap the ingredient or category you want to add. A checkmark confirms your selection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Add">
    Tap **Add** to save it to your sensitivity list. The ingredient or category now appears on your Allergies & Sensitivities screen and is active for all future scans.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What happens when a flagged ingredient is found

When you scan a product that contains an ingredient or category from your sensitivity list:

- A **red alert banner** appears at the very top of your scan results, before the ingredient breakdown or safety score. It lists every matched ingredient by name.
- Each matched ingredient in the full ingredient list is highlighted and labeled with a **"Personal Alert"** badge so you can see exactly where it appears in the formula.
- The alert persists when you save the product to your library — re-opening a saved product with a matched sensitivity always shows the alert.

<Warning>
  Allergies & Sensitivities is a tool for tracking personal preferences and ingredients you've chosen to avoid — it is not a diagnostic tool. The app cannot confirm whether you are medically allergic to a specific ingredient. If you have experienced a confirmed allergic reaction or have been diagnosed with a contact allergy, consult a doctor or board-certified dermatologist. Do not use this feature as a substitute for professional medical advice.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  If a dermatologist or allergist has given you a list of ingredients to avoid — for example, after a patch test — add each ingredient to your sensitivity list by INCI name. This way, every product you scan is automatically checked against your doctor's recommendations.
</Tip>
