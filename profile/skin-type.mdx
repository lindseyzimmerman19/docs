Your skin type determines which ingredients the app flags as relevant, which warnings surface first, and how your overall product score is weighted. Setting it accurately means every scan you run reflects what actually matters for your skin — not a generic checklist.

## Skin types supported

The app supports six skin types. You can select more than one if your skin fits multiple descriptions.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Normal" icon="circle-check">
    Balanced moisture levels with few sensitivities. Most ingredients are well-tolerated.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Dry" icon="droplet-slash">
    Prone to flaking and tightness. The app flags alcohol-heavy formulas and highlights hydrating ingredients.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Oily" icon="droplets">
    Excess sebum production. The app prioritizes non-comedogenic ingredient checks and flags pore-clogging compounds.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Combination" icon="circle-half-stroke">
    Oily T-zone with drier cheeks. The app flags both comedogenic ingredients and harsh astringents.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sensitive" icon="shield-exclamation">
    Easily irritated. Selecting this type adds extra flags for fragrance, essential oils, and common irritants.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Acne-Prone" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Benefits from non-comedogenic, non-pore-clogging formulas. The app highlights ingredients known to trigger breakouts.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Set your skin type

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your Profile tab">
    Tap the **Profile** icon in the bottom navigation bar to open your profile settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap 'Skin Type'">
    Under the "My Skin" section, tap **Skin Type** to open the selection screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your type or types">
    Tap one or more skin types that describe your skin. Each selected type is highlighted with a checkmark. You can combine types — for example, Combination and Acne-Prone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Save">
    Tap **Save** to apply your selection. Your profile updates immediately, and all future scans reflect your chosen skin types.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What the Sensitive skin type adds

Selecting **Sensitive** as one of your skin types activates an expanded set of ingredient checks beyond the standard analysis. The app adds prominent flags for:

- Synthetic and natural fragrances (including fragrance blends listed as "parfum" or "aroma")
- Essential oils, which can cause sensitization even in small amounts
- Common irritants such as alcohol denat., menthol, and witch hazel
- Preservatives with a higher irritation profile, including methylisothiazolinone and formaldehyde releasers

These flags appear in your scan results alongside your standard ingredient breakdown.

<Note>
  Your skin type affects scan results in three concrete ways: it changes the priority ordering of flagged concerns (most relevant to your skin type surface first), adds personalized warning badges to specific ingredients, and adjusts the weighting used to calculate your overall product safety score. A product that scores well for Normal skin may score lower for Sensitive skin if it contains fragrance or high-irritation preservatives.
</Note>

<Tip>
  You can update your skin type at any time from your Profile tab. When you do, any products you've previously saved to your library are automatically re-evaluated against your updated profile — the next time you open a saved product, its badges, warnings, and score reflect your current settings.
</Tip>
